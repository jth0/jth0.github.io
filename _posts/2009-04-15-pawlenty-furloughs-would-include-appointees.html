On the topic of mandatory furloughs for state employees, mediation writes:

Two things are totally awesome about furloughs.  First, the very idea of a furlough is that you have employees that are being paid for non-essential work.  On top of that, if you’ve been working somewhere for more that 6-8 years, which is the case with many, many Boomers in all facets of the economy, you have a ton of vacation time.  Like a “the European countries are jealous” amount.  And you’re at 4 weeks of vacation and have to not work for 48 days, you’re pretty much part time.

Unfortunately, this plan would be used to cover T-Paw & The Legislature’s fiscal malfeasance by forcing state employees, who have nothing to do with the budget apart from being tax-paying citizens, to use their hard-earned vacation. Potentially, without regard to whether they’re essential. (The definition of essential, by the way, means you can’t do anything but try to keep the lights on.)

Oh, and for those in my boat, with the state less than six years or those who actually use their vacation time, well, we’d get fucked. Awesome.
