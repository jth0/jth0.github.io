I have made the jump to Mac and OS X … I was wondering what anyone would suggest for resources or books to get under the hood of OS X.

I’ve fielded this question several times in the past few weeks and figured I’d post my response here for posterity and reference.

Apple’s training is a really good resource, and even if some of your mentors suck, it’s $99. For one course a week. For a year. And that’s actually $79 if you are still in school (or at least still have your student id ;) Oh, and that training is good for Apple’s pro apps like Aperture, too.

My “short” list of third-party apps to check out:

Quicksilver rocks. Adium is much nicer [IMO] than iChat. Aperture 2 rocks. Firefox 3 beta is more stable and faster than FF2 on mac. Office 2008 is more stable and about as fast as 2004. If you’re a twitterer, twitterific is nice. If you don’t have a decent external hard drive, use the Disk Utility (inside Applications/Utilities) to resize your primary partition and leave yourself 75-100GB for a second partition to use with Time Machine backups. SuperDuper! is great for taking a bootable backup. [These go for all users, regardless of Mac, Win or Linux.] Use Mozy, iBackup, or some similar over-the-wire for off-site backup.

Hard disk errors still happen. Apple’s disk utilities is more or less fsck with a few more capabilities. But it’s not rock-solid. If/when you have hdd issues, DiskWarrior will probably fix it. Worth having on-hand for preventative maintenance.
