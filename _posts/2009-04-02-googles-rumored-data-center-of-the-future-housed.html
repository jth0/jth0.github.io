Google’s rumored data center of the future, housed in a standard shipping container, is now history. It turns out they’ve been using these since 2005. They’re packed full of simply-designed servers (1,160/container) and highly portable due to the container’s ease of transport on rail, at sea or over the road. Each server uses a dual-CPU board from Gigabyte (and maybe others) with 8 slots of RAM. There’s a good chance that new containers have newer equipment, but that’s not clear. Perhaps the most interesting note unveiled yesterday: each server has an on-board 12V battery in case of power loss. Evidently, this choice makes battery backup significantly cheaper than a giant UPS with a slew of batteries.
